#+title: Mindmapping illuminates one's personal evolution.
So does journaling, of course. But reading your old journal entries are typically only done with the express purpose of understanding that past self, and hence is done less often. Your knowledge base, by contrast, is something you will find yourself consulting often, for practical reasons. And one side effect of those frequent consultations is to notice how your mind has evolved -- both how smart you used to be (which can be hard to appreciate), and how different your beliefs and choices are now.
